What equipment can disability grants actually fund?

More than you probably think

Written by Andy Hutton, founder of Theo's Fight  ·  Dad to Theo, aged 8, cerebral palsy

One of the most common things we hear from families is this: "I didn't think we could apply for that."

Disability grants cover a much wider range of equipment than most families realise. If you've been assuming a piece of equipment is out of reach financially — or that no grant would cover it — this article is for you.

The obvious ones

Wheelchairs, communication devices, standing frames, orthotics. Most families know grants exist for these. If you need help finding them — that's exactly what Theo's Fight is for.

The ones that surprise people

This is where it gets interesting. Grants can potentially fund:

🌈Sensory equipment — lighting, swings, weighted blankets, bubble tubes
🚲Adapted bikes and trikes — like Theo's Tomcat
🛏️Specialist beds and sleep systems
🚿Bathroom adaptations — shower chairs, specialist baths, wet rooms
🌳Garden equipment — raised beds, adapted play, outdoor sensory spaces
🫧White goods — washing machines and tumble dryers
💻Computers and tablets — for education and independence
✈️Family breaks and respite holidays
🚗Specialist car seats and harnesses
👟Specialist clothing and footwear

The rule of thumb

If it directly improves your child's quality of life, supports their development, or gives your family essential respite — it's worth searching for a grant. The worst anyone can say is no.

One thing to know

Different grants fund different things. A grant that covers communication devices won't necessarily cover garden equipment. That's why searching specifically — with your child's details and the exact equipment you need — gives you a much better result than searching generally.

That's what Theo's Fight does. Tell us what you need and we'll find what matches. Free, always. 💙

⚠️ Grant eligibility varies by organisation and changes regularly. Always verify directly with each charity before applying.
